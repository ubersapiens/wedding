Getting there

Fly

Flights from Paris and major European capitals take you directly to Napoleon Bonaparte airport in Ajaccio. Find the cheapest. Fast!

Sail

If plane doesn't cut it, get to Marseille, Nice or Toulon to then grab the Corsica Ferry. Brace yourself for the sceneries.

Swim

Only 100km separate Corsica from European mainland, so if you have it in you, send your bags by post and put on your goggles. No sharks, guaranteed.

Renting a car is a DEFINITE MUST. From Ajaccio to your hotel. From your hotel to the village. And more.

Things to do

1

Trekking

Corsica is filled with some of the planet's most mountainous landscapes and is home to the legendary GR20. We will be doing one major hike on Wednesday August 26 (Lac de Creno, Balade de Muna, Girolata or parts of the GR20)

2

Football

Since their is people coming from all over we will be organizing a mini World Cup on Thursday August 27 in the village's football field, so pack your Messi and Ronaldo jerseys.

3

Diving and snorkeling

For those who like jumping into the water their are a thousand and one spots to check out corals, fish and thongs. Check out this link for more information.

4

Canyoning

The area where we will be getting married is filled with large turquoise water holes, red diagonal walls of red rock and perfect spots for sliding all day. More info on Les Escapades.

5

Parapente

With some of the most beautiful beaches in the Mediterranean and magic gusts of wind, the Golf of Sagone is the perfect spot for flying.

6

Boat trips

The Calanques de Piana is one of Corsica's most popular spots. Imagine tall walls of rock descending directly down to transparent water. Now imagine seeing that on a beautiful boat.
